About Nepal

Nepal is a country of strong contrasts: high mountains and low plains, quiet villages and busy cities, ancient temples and modern life. This page gives a short, clear overview.

Country Overview

Nepal is a landlocked country in South Asia, located between India and China. The country is best known for the Himalayan range, which includes Mount Everest, the world’s highest peak. Even with its small area, Nepal has a wide variety of landscapes and climates.

Many ethnic groups live in Nepal, each with its own language, festivals and traditions. Hinduism and Buddhism are the main religions, and their influence can be seen in architecture, art and daily life.

Short History Snapshot

For centuries, the Kathmandu Valley was home to several small kingdoms, which built impressive palaces and temples. In the 18th century, King Prithvi Narayan Shah unified many of these areas into one kingdom, which later became modern Nepal.

Nepal remained independent during the colonial period but maintained close ties with neighbours. In the 20th and 21st century, the country went through big political changes, moving from monarchy towards a federal democratic republic.

Culture & Religion

Daily life in Nepal is closely connected to religion and tradition. Temples, stupas and monasteries are common meeting places, and many families take part in festivals throughout the year.

  • Dashain: Major Hindu festival celebrating family and victory of good over evil.
  • Tihar: Festival of lights, honouring animals, siblings and the goddess of wealth.
  • Buddhist rituals: Prayer flags, mani wheels and meditation are common in many regions.

Food & Daily Life

Traditional Nepali food is simple but filling, often based on rice, lentils and seasonal vegetables. Street stalls and small family restaurants serve both local dishes and international options.

  • Dal bhat: Classic combination of rice, lentil soup, vegetables and pickles.
  • Momo: Steamed or fried dumplings filled with vegetables or meat.
  • Chiyaa (tea): Sweet milk tea often shared with friends and guests.

Three Main Regions of Nepal

A simple way to understand Nepal is to look at it from south to north: Terai, Hills and Himalayas.

Region Landscape Examples
Terai Flat, hot lowland plains with agriculture and jungles. Biratnagar, Janakpur, Nepalgunj, Chitwan
Hills Green hills, valleys and small towns with mild climate. Kathmandu Valley, Pokhara, Palpa
Himalayas High mountains and remote villages with trekking routes. Everest Region, Annapurna Region, Mustang
