Scenes from Nepal

Explore a few typical views from Nepal: historic squares, lakeside walks, mountain trails and colourful festivals. Replace these example images with your own photos when you develop the project further.

Cities & Heritage

The cities in Nepal mix old and new – you can find centuries-old temples next to modern shops and busy traffic.

Kathmandu Durbar Square

A UNESCO World Heritage site with palaces, courtyards and temples. Early morning and late afternoon are calm and atmospheric.

Patan

Known for its beautiful Newari architecture, narrow lanes and traditional metal and wood crafts.

Bhaktapur

An old city with red-brick houses, pottery squares and impressive temples, a great place for slow walks and photos.

Nature & Trekking

Even short walks just outside cities can give mountain views, small villages and quiet landscapes.

Mountain Trails

Well-known routes vary from easy one-day hikes to long adventures. Local guides and tea houses support many treks.

Phewa Lake, Pokhara

Calm waters, colourful boats and cafés create a relaxing atmosphere with mountain views on clear days.

Hilltop Viewpoints

Short early-morning hikes to nearby hills often reward you with sea-of-clouds views and golden light on the peaks.

Culture, People & Festivals

People in Nepal are generally friendly and curious about visitors. Festivals are good moments to experience music, colours and local traditions.

Dashain & Tihar

Homes and streets are decorated, families visit each other and people receive blessings from elders.

Stupas & Prayer Flags

Colourful flags and circular walking paths around stupas are common ways of showing respect and seeking good luck.

Local Markets

Fresh vegetables, spices and everyday goods fill lively markets where you can observe daily life and buy simple snacks.
