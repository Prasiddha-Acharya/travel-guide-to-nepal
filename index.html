Discover Nepal

Nepal is a small country between India and China, famous for the Himalayas, colourful culture and friendly people. This simple travel guide gives you an overview of places to visit, things to do, and tips for your first trip.

Start your journey

Why Visit Nepal?

Whether you love mountains, culture, food or wildlife, Nepal offers something special for every traveller. Here are a few reasons why people fall in love with this country.

Majestic Nature

From the snowy peaks of Everest to green hills and rivers, Nepal is full of trekking routes, viewpoints and peaceful landscapes.

Rich Culture

Temples, festivals, traditional music and food make Nepal a unique destination for travellers who like culture and history.

Vibrant Cities

Kathmandu and Pokhara are lively cities with markets, lakeside cafés, heritage sites and many options for day trips.

Quick Facts About

These facts help you get a basic picture before planning your route.

Capital City Kathmandu
Official Language Nepali (English widely used in tourism)
Currency Nepalese Rupee (NPR)
Best Time to Visit October–November and March–April
Main Attractions Himalayas, Kathmandu Valley, Pokhara, Lumbini, Chitwan National Park

Top 5 Experiences for First-Time Visitors

  1. Walk around the old squares of Kathmandu, Patan or Bhaktapur in the evening.
  2. Enjoy sunrise views of the Himalayas from a hill station or trekking viewpoint.
  3. Spend a calm afternoon by Phewa Lake in Pokhara, renting a small boat.
  4. Visit a local temple or stupa and observe daily rituals respectfully.
  5. Try traditional dishes like momo, dal bhat and Newari snacks.

Choose Your Travel Style

Adventure & Trekking

Ideal for travellers who enjoy hiking and outdoor challenges. Popular routes include Everest Region, Annapurna Circuit and short hikes near Pokhara.

Cultural City Break

Stay in Kathmandu Valley, explore temples, museums and local cafés, and join walking tours in narrow streets and courtyards.

Relaxed Nature Trip

Combine quiet days in Pokhara with a visit to Chitwan or another national park for light walks, boat rides and wildlife watching.
